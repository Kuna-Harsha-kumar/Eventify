WHY WATCH A SONDHEIM CELEBRATION WITH KEVIN STITES?

ONCE UPON A TIME

"A Sondheim Celebration with Kevin Stites" is a concert film that features a performance of several iconic songs from the musical theater works of composer and lyricist Stephen Sondheim. The concert was recorded live at the Carnegie Hall in New York City in 1992. In addition to the performances, the film also features interviews with Sondheim, Stites, and some of the cast members, as well as archival footage of Sondheim's earlier work. The film is a celebration of Sondheim's contributions to musical theater and showcases his skillful lyrics and intricate compositions. It is a must-see for fans of Sondheim's work and for anyone who appreciates the artistry and beauty of musical theater.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

Kevin Stites conducts the New York Philharmonic Orchestra and leads a cast of Broadway stars, including Bernadette Peters, Patti LuPone, Barbara Cook, and George Hearn, in performances of classic Sondheim songs such as "Send in the Clowns," "Being Alive," and "Losing My Mind."
