WHY WATCH LES MISERABLES?

ONCE UPON A TIME

"Les Miserables" is a popular musical based on the classic novel of the same name by Victor Hugo. The musical is known for its powerful score, which includes songs such as "I Dreamed a Dream," "On My Own," and "One Day More." It has been performed in over 40 countries and translated into 22 languages, making it one of the most successful musicals of all time. The themes of love, sacrifice, and redemption continue to resonate with audiences around the world.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

The story takes place in 19th-century France and follows the life of Jean Valjean, a French peasant who is imprisoned for stealing a loaf of bread to feed his sister's starving child. After serving 19 years in prison, Valjean is released on parole but is constantly pursued by the ruthless police inspector Javert. Valjean breaks his parole and begins a new life under a false identity, becoming a successful businessman and mayor of a small town. However, Javert continues to track him down, leading to a series of events that test Valjean's moral character and force him to confront his past. The story also includes several other characters, such as Fantine, a single mother who is forced into prostitution to provide for her daughter Cosette; Marius Pontmercy, a young revolutionary who falls in love with Cosette; and Eponine, a streetwise girl who has unrequited feelings for Marius.
